# Applied Discrete Mathematics

It introduces people to some powerful linear algebra and probabilistic methods that have been used with striking success in discrete mathematics, and covers some of the most fundamental and beautiful results obtained by these methods.

> Linear algebra method: rank argument, eigenvalue technique, polynomial technique, general position method.

[Lecture note (Rank Argument)](https://galobelwang.github.io/file/LinearAlgebraMethodRankArgument.pdf), includes

1. (Counting clubs in Oddtown) Theorem: In a town of $n$ citizens, no more than $n$ clubs can be formed under rules: "each club should have an odd number of members" and "each pair of clubs should have an even number of members in common".
2. (Point sets in \(\mathbb{R}^n\) with Equal Distance) Theorem: Let \(a_1, a_2, \ddots, a_m\) be points in \(\mathbb{R}^n\) such that the pairwise Euclidean distances of \(a_1\)'s are all equal. Then \( m \leq n+1 \).
