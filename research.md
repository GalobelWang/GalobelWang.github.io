# GCMC conjecture

## Information Theory with Algebraic Geometry

> In the past decade, [June Huh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/June_Huh) solved several long-standing open problems on log-concave sequences in combinatorics. The ground-breaking techniques developed in those work are from algebraic geometry: "We believe that behind any log-concave sequence that appears in nature there is such a Hodge structure responsible for the log-concavity".

> A function is called completely monotone if its derivatives alternate in signs, A fundamental conjecture in mathematical physics and Shannon information theory is on the **[complete monotonicity of Gaussian distribution (GCMC)](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2208.13108)**, which states that the [Fisher information](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fisher_information) of random variables X+Z is [completely monotone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Absolutely_and_completely_monotonic_functions_and_sequences) in t, where Z is a [Gaussian noise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaussian_noise) with variance t, X and Z are independent.

> Inspired by the algebraic geometry method introduced by [June Huh](https://web.math.princeton.edu/~huh/), GCMC is reformulated in the form of a log-convex sequence. In general, a completely monotone function can admit a log-convex sequence and a log-convex sequence can further induce a log-concave sequence. The new formulation may guide GCMC to the marvelous temple of algebraic geometry.

- [Differential manifold and Hodge theory](https://galobelwang.github.io/file/Differential_manifold_and_Hodge_theory.pdf)
- [Hodge theory for matroids](https://galobelwang.github.io/file/Hodge_theory_for_matroids.pdf)

# Integrable Probability

> **Integrable probability** is an area of research at the interface between probability theory, mathematical physics, combinatorics and representation theory. It refers to the study of probabilistic models that are exactly solvable.

> The notion of **exact solvability** or **integrability** is somewhat vague. A model is called exactly solvable when observables of interest can be computed by a formula involving well-known functions (rational functions, exp, sin, Γ), so that the complexity of the formula does not increase as parameters go to ∞.
